Ulster is one of the four historical provinces which make up the island of Ireland. The others are Leinster, Munster and Connacht.

There are nine counties in Ulster. Three are in the Republic of Ireland. The other six counties make up all of Northern Ireland, part of the United Kingdom.

The name "Ulster" is also sometimes used to mean Northern Ireland, even though Northern Ireland is only part of Ulster.

The counties are:

In the Republic of Ireland:

  • County Cavan
  • County Donegal
  • County Monaghan

In Northern Ireland:

  • County Antrim
  • County Armagh
  • County Down
  • County Fermanagh
  • County Londonderry
  • County Tyrone
